Technology & Innovation Technological innovation is reshaping the sector. Blockchain and stablecoin regulations are being explored in Europe to improve the traceability and credibility of renewable electricity consumption, potentially unlocking new investment models. International collaboration, such as UNDP and UNECE support in emerging regions, is helping to transfer knowledge and accelerate decarbonization.

Heat pump and EV adoption continue to rise, though growth rates are slowing as markets mature.

Funding & Projects Global renewable investment reached USD 728 billion in 2024, up 8% from the previous year but with a notable slowdown, particularly in wind. Investments in road transport infrastructure grew by 20%, surpassing those in renewables, while heat pump investment declined slightly.

Risks & Constraints Key risks include grid congestion, policy uncertainty, and tightening access to finance in several markets. Social acceptance is a growing concern, as seen in regions like Sardinia, where local opposition to wind projects has intensified. In the US, slower renewable growth has led utilities to rely more on natural gas, reflecting the complex balance between decarbonization and grid reliability.
