Executive Overview The global energy sector is undergoing rapid transformation, driven by rising demand, decarbonization goals, and the need for resilient infrastructure. While investments in renewables and clean technologies are at record levels, progress toward 2030 targets remains uneven. Key challenges include infrastructure bottlenecks, regional disparities, and the need for integrated policy and finance.

Analysis from BCG indicates that commercially ready decarbonization technologies could address nearly two-thirds of global energy-related emissions if policy, security, and climate objectives are advanced together. 4 trillion invested in the energy transition in 2024, growth is slowing and is not on pace to triple renewable power capacity by 2030. Areas like energy efficiency, green hydrogen, and storage require significant scale-up. Regional investment remains uneven, with energy access lagging in many parts of the world.

Without integrated grids, Europe risks missing climate and competitiveness goals. Globally, renewables growth is uneven, and regional disparities in investment and energy access persist. Outlook Achieving energy transition goals will require coordinated action across technology, policy, and finance. Integrated grids, expanded renewables, and innovative solutions like SAF and hydrogen mobility are essential. Policymakers and industry must prioritize infrastructure investment, market integration, and equitable access to ensure a secure, affordable, and sustainable energy future.
